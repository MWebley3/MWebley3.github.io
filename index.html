KMS Superstore Sales Analysis (2009-2012)

About

  • Comprehensive analysis of KMS Superstore 2009 - 2012 sales performance across different regions and customer segments, enabling stakeholders to drive business growth and increase profits.

Tools Used

  • Microsoft Excel: For data storage and organization.
  • PowerBI: For creating the interactive dashboard, enabling dynamic data visualization.

Observations

  • The average customer buys 26 items per visit.
  • The average sale is $1,780 with a profit of $181.

Conclusion

  • To maximize profits, focus on main profitable segments, small businesses and corporate, both generating an average profit of $192 and $195 respectively, with Ontario and Prairie being the two in-demand regions.

TaTa Forage Job Stimulation: Global Mart 2011 Sales Dashboard

About

  • This project aimed to use data visualization for the CEO and CMO of Global Mart, helping them sustain sales growth using historical data from their best performing year, 2011, by identifying the top ten customers and market areas

Tools Used

  • Microsoft Excel: For data cleaning and preparation.
  • Tableau: For data visualization and creating interactive dashboard.

Observations

  • Netherlands, EIRE(Ireland), France, and Germany were countries driving quanitity demand and revenue growth.
  • Seasonality in sales shown from September - November with revenue above $1,000,000 each month.

Conclusion

  • Increase marketing efforts outside peak months, and implement targeted strategies in underperforming regions to boost overall sales.

Excel Visualization: CentralEmporium July 2021 Sales Dashboard

About

  • The goal of this project was to gain insights on consumer preferences, product demand dynamics, and regional market trends. The sales data encompassed transactions from two distinct countries, South Africa and Nigeria.

Tools Used

  • Microsoft Excel: For data cleaning, preparation, dashboarding, and analysis using pivot tables, IFs function, currency conversion, filters, and charts.

Observations

  • South Africa had $1,649,348 in total sales with 7,587 quantities sold.
  • Home care was the most sold category with 49.19% of percent share of sales.

Conclusion

  • Prioritizing high-demand products boosts sales across countries, while tailored region-specific strategies enhance overall business success.
