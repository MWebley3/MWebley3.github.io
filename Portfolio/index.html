Understanding the Maternal Death Crisis in the U.S.

About

  • Analyzed CDC WONDER maternal mortality data (2018–2023) using Python, Claude, and Google Colab. Delivered insights by demographic and region to support public health equity efforts. Created a visual report in Gamma summarizing key trends and actionable recommendations.

Tools Used

  • Gamma: For storytelling and presenting.
  • Google Colab: For data cleaning, analysis, and visualization using python.
  • Claude: For insights and recommendations overview.

Observations

  • Black mothers were 3 times more likely to die from maternal related deaths than white mothers.
  • Black and white mothers with a GED/High school diploma are most impacted by maternal related deaths.
  • Mothers who are from the South faced the highest regional risk of dying from a pregnancy related death, especially those 25-39 years old.

Conclusion

  • These findings underscore the urgent need for equity-focused interventions in maternal health. Addressing racial, education, age, and regional disparities is essential to reducing preventable maternal deaths.

Sales Analysis (2009-2012)

About

  • Comprehensive analysis of 2009 - 2012 sales performance across different regions and customer segments, enabling stakeholders to drive business growth and increase profits.

Tools Used

  • PowerBI: For creating the interactive dashboard, enabling dynamic data visualization.
  • Microsoft Excel: For data storage and organization.

Observations

  • The average customer buys 26 items per visit.
  • The average sale is $1,780 with a profit of $181.

Conclusion

  • To maximize profits, focus on main profitable segments, small businesses and corporate, both generating an average profit of $192 and $195 respectively, with Ontario and Prairie being the two in-demand regions.
